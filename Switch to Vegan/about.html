How it all began

A video I
couldn't ignore

It's not a conversation, a book, or a health scare. It was a video. That kind you don't expect. Showing how animals are treated inside the dairy industry. Not in a distant factory. From local suppliers. The kind most people, including me, always thought were fine.

I could not process what I watched and continue doing what I was doing. So I made a decision to want to no longer contribute to it. That decision was simple. Everything that came after it was not.

"I didn't go vegan. I started trying to go vegan. Those felt like two very different things — and that set my pace."

The first few weeks were truly confusing. I didn't have ideas about what to eat on a morning. I didn't feel comfortable explaining it on a dinner table. Every recommendation I found online clashed with the previous one. I knew I didn't want to do what I didn't want to do — I just had to figure out how to do it.

That confusion — and having to do it alone, imperfect and at my own pace — led to this website.

The beginning is never so dramatic. It is an unnoticeable change of just noticing something you just can't help noticing.

The real journey

Unnoticeable. Imperfect.
And it worked.

The real tool

"Not today. Maybe tomorrow."

The most significant thing I observed in myself: the harder I told myself "I can never have this again," the more my mind fixated on that very thing. For me, combating it head-on made it worse. So I chose to stay away. When I wanted an ice cream, I expressed — not today — maybe tomorrow. Tomorrow came, and I told myself the same thing. Over weeks, the craving was less intense. It dissolved away — instead of going so far as to make me feel like I was deprived, it started feeling like a choice I was making, in my own way, at my own time and for my own little reasons.

1

Not ordering dairy outside first

Restaurants gave me option without disturbing anything at home. Small, private decision — and for me, that felt so much easier to follow than even the most dramatic statement.

2

Dairy reduced step-by-step — not all at once

All at once with cheese and desserts, one choice at a time. I didn't declare anything. I simply took to choosing differently, occasionally at first and then more and more often. Success without performance.

3

Dairy omitted at home — over many months

It was not food that was difficult — it was habit. Tea in the morning. A familiar comfort. I had to identify alternatives I actually liked and not only tolerated — for any of it to be sustainable.

4

Actual research started — and I stopped reading wellness blogs

Switching from chosen wellness blogs to peer-reviewed materials — registered dietitians, well-known institutions, research — made nutrition feel much less unmanageable and much more steerable. I stopped feeling overwhelmed and started feeling curious.

5

Realised veganism is not just about food

Leather, silk, honey and cosmetics tested on animals — most initial ideas do not know this at the beginning. I did not either. That's not being wrong. That is learning through stages, over a period of time, without a deadline.

The reason I created this platform

I was in need of something
that didn't exist yet.

The Indian experience has specific hurdles that most of the vegan literature I came across failed to address — as it wasn't designed considering this background. For me, responses related to nutrition felt a bit much. Vegan options demanded high prices and difficult accessibility. Indian dishes revolve around dairy in terms of familiarity that is difficult to manipulate. Social occasions have a significance that extends beyond just the food. And identifying whose articles on the internet one can genuinely trust took greater push from me than I had left when I was so confused.

"I did not begin this with the aim of being the pioneer among vegans. I just aimed to serve the space I did miss — a sane, practical, India-centred resource, and so began building one."

I did not find this easy. Especially ideas which felt almost impossible to combat. About craving for ice cream you do not find rational explanations for. About not knowing how to get enough protein to train in the gym. About not being able to buy cost-effective almond milk. About all the clutter I read about actual facts like B12, omega-3, and iron — none of which suited each other. About Indian family dinners. About not finding a single repository of recipes which cooked with what you actually found in an Indian kitchen, in an Indian budget.

Every gadget, guide and article in this website originated from just one of those specific difficulties I encountered. A recipe masterplanner tailor-made to Indian ingredients. Nutrition resources that referenced research documents, degrees and tables rather than opinions. Cost-conscious alternatives as and where I was able to source them. Upfront records of difficult questions I encountered. Not inspiration. Purely starting points.

This platform is not about fixing oneself. It is not about shame. It is not about changing anyone's heart — just attempting to make it a little uncomplicated for individuals who are somewhere in the middle of searching for it, precisely how I was.

What this place stands for

Five things
that won't
change here.

Not marketing copy. The actual principles this site was built around — and the things I wish I'd found earlier.

01

No shame. Not ever.

You will never be made to feel guilty for where you are. Slow transitions count. Slipping up counts. Starting again counts. All of it counts.

02

Research over opinion.

Where possible, nutrition information on this site links back to published research rather than personal opinion. When something is uncertain or debated, the aim is to say so clearly rather than overstate what's known.

03

India first, always.

Grocery lists, recipes, protein sources, social situations — built around Indian kitchens, Indian budgets, Indian families, and Indian culture. Not retrofitted from elsewhere.

04

Practical over inspirational.

Inspiration is easy to find. A clear, specific answer on a Tuesday evening when you don't know what to cook is much harder. That's what this tries to be.

05

Your pace. Not anyone else's.

There is no right speed. No correct version. The only thing that matters is that you keep going — in whatever way fits your life, your family, your reality.

All that this site seeks to be honest about

What this site tries
to be honest about.

I am not a clinical expert — a researcher or a doctor. I am an individual who went through this transition, fell by the wayside, intensely sought better information and wanted to help share the factors that eased my move. Here is what I attempt as a principle to approach content generated for this website — and how its limitations lay.

1

All references to authentic documents

Wherever nutrition data is shared, I aim to link it back to the original report from a source such as the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, Harvard T.H. Chan or NHS — so that you may view the original document and not just hear me talk.

2

Aims to be honest when topics are still maturing

A lot of nutrition science as far as we present it is genuinely disputed or still in a framework of progression. The goal is to communicate "this topic is currently in progress" as accurately as I can into the write-up and prevent exaggerating or giving false confidence.

3

Updates the pages when information improves

Knowledge around us is always advancing. I hope to review any web content as I realise it is stale. However, some pages may be less evidence-based rather than more in the beginning. If you spot any inaccuracies or can present research I do not seem to have come across, I would be grateful.

4

Not a replacement for professional counselling

This is a personal blog, not a clinical webpage. If you have a medical condition, are expecting, or require personal dietary input, consult with a registered dietitian or an experienced health practitioner — not a blog.

i

To be completely clear: I am not an experienced health practitioner, a researcher or dietician. I am a person who just transitioned and became overwhelmed by a plethora of more likely confusing information — looked for better advice, and hope to give a starting point for realising what goes into finding strict information as a first step. Treat this material as a stepping off point and keep talking to expert professionals for anything that concerns health.
